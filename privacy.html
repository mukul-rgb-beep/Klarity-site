Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 8, 2025

Klarity does not collect, store, or transmit any of your personal data. All disk analysis happens entirely on your Mac. We have no servers, no analytics, and no tracking.

What Data Klarity Accesses

Klarity reads file and folder metadata (names, sizes, and directory structure) from the disk volumes you choose to scan. This information is used solely to generate the visual analysis displayed within the app.

What Data We Collect

None. Klarity collects zero personal data. Specifically:

  • No file contents are read — only names and sizes
  • No data is transmitted over the internet
  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind
  • No user accounts or sign-in required
  • No cookies, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting
  • No third-party SDKs or advertising frameworks

Data Storage

Your scan results and app preferences (such as your chosen theme and visualization mode) are stored locally on your Mac using standard macOS mechanisms (UserDefaults). This data never leaves your device.

Third-Party Services

Klarity does not integrate with any third-party services, analytics platforms, or advertising networks. The app functions entirely offline.

Children's Privacy

Klarity does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13. Since no data is collected or transmitted, there are no age-related privacy concerns.

macOS Permissions

Klarity may request the following macOS permissions:

  • Full Disk Access (optional): Required to scan system-protected directories. Without this permission, Klarity can still scan your home folder and most user-accessible locations.

These permissions are handled entirely by macOS and are revocable at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever update this privacy policy, changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Given that Klarity collects no data, significant changes are unlikely.
